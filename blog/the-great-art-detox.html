The Great Art Detox

May 11, 2025

We're at the precipice of transformation.

With AI-generated images flooding traditional media, marketing material and social media feeds, many believe that this is the death of human creativity.

I'd argue the very opposite.

I'd argue that this presents a unique opportunity for artists and other creatives to finally stand out from the crowd of commercial and institutional sludge.

The contemporary art world has been split into two:

Commodified Art—platforms like Fiverr and ArtStation created an ecosystem of purely commercial work—generic logos, stock illustrations, and formulaic digital paintings with no value beyond their price tag.

Institutional Art—In today's institutional art world, conceptual work dominates major museums, galleries and academic circles. Curators, critics and art foundations increasingly elevate theoretical frameworks above technical execution. These works cloak themselves with the apparence of profoundity, but are often without substance and supported only by academic jargon and institutional validation.

Caught between these extremes, genuine artistic vision struggles to breathe.

When these voices do emerge, they're quickly drowned out by the sheer volume of derivative work or co-opted by elite institutions more interested in theoretical frameworks than raw, creative power.

This is where I believe AI is a game changer—for the better.

When anyone can generate "art" with a prompt, work by artists with unique perspectives and a distinctive vision becomes much more valuable, meaningful and immediately identifiable.

While AI can imitate surface-level aesthetics very well, technical mastery and creativity will always be out of reach to it. AI "creates" by mixing patterns from its training data, not by "creating" anything from scratch.

Machines miss the deeper intention and emotional undertone that makes great art…great.

As this transformation accelerates, I believe we'll see a fundamental shift in how art is valued:

Patronage will re-flourish—Artists who possess distinctive voices will find support through funding models where supporters invest in the creator rather than purchasing individual works. With Patreon, this has become an increasingly popular way for individuals and businesses to support their favorite artists.

Authenticity will become a new currency—Verifying that a piece of art was created by a human will become increasingly important and significantly improve their worth.

Community will take center stage—Artists who build genuine connections with their audiences are positioned to thrive.

The human story behind the art and the experiences that shaped it will become as important as the visual output itself.

For new and established artists, these are my suggestions:

Cultivate your unique voice—develop works that could only have come from your specific perspective and lived experience.

Embrace technology thoughtfully—consider how AI tools might free you from the most basic aspects of creation to focus on the more unique aspects of it.

Build direct relationships with your audience—human connection between artists and viewers will become invaluable in an AI-saturated world.

Make your humanity visible by showing the journey of creation—the struggles, decisions, and breakthroughs that AI cannot replicate.

This AI revolution in the art world will be painful, but transformative.

Photography freed painters to explore new artistic movements beyond realistic representations and I believe AI will free human artists from the commercial pressures to conform and repeat.

The future belongs to those with truly distinctive voices—those who create works that could never be conceived by a machine because they emerge from uniquely human experiences of joy, suffering, love, and wonder.

These artists will no longer compete with the white noise of mass-produced commercial imagery. Instead, they'll stand as beacons of authentic human creativity in a world increasingly saturated with algorithmic outputs.

A great detox has begun. And true artists will emerge stronger than ever before.
